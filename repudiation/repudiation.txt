- Briefly explain the vulnerability and why the secure version is better at preventing repudiation than the insecure version.
- What additional security mechanisms can you incorporate to prevent such attacks?

A repudiation threat arises when someone involved in a transaction denies their participation or the details of the transaction itself. This can be problematic, especially in legal or financial contexts where a record of agreement is crucial.

The secure.js version has a log which tracks what changes were made in addition to making the changes, so it's possible to verify when events occurred.
